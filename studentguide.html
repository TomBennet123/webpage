Our Mission & Vision

Our objective is to explore new areas of science while maintaining the conventional values of engineering education and upholding the highest national and international academic standards. Our mission is to educate students with an extensive understanding of their courses and crucial transferable skills and traits that will help them succeed in the workplace. We aim to deliver courses that meet the needs of industry and play a crucial role in creating a generation of engineers who will lead the development of innovative and sustainable technologies.

The undergraduate programme of the Faculty provides excellent professional education in the following areas: water operation engineering , environmental sciences, and environmental engineering. Master's courses are taught in six major subject areas: materials engineering, environmental engineering, chemical engineering, chemistry, and environmental sciences. The Faculty welcomes applicants for PhD programs in a range of disciplines in the Doctoral School of Chemistry and Environmental Sciences and the Doctoral School of Chemical Engineering and Materials Science.

The Faculty of Engineering provides a solid academic and practice-oriented education. Our aim is not just to discuss sustainability, but to make it widely accessible through innovative solutions that can be applied in practice, both at the regional and global levels. The Faculty of Engineering has a long tradition of hosting and supporting international students. Therefore, we aim to offer you the best international experience possible.

Guide for Current Students

Getting to the University Campuses

Hungary has one international airport, the Liszt Ferenc International Airport in Budapest, which is a well-connected transportation point. Then students have 3 public transport routes to choose from when getting from Budapest to the Campuses.

Registration

Once you have obtained your visa and entered Hungary, you will need to register at your university. The university reserves the first week of each new academic year for enrolment. It would be advisable to check the student office and the institution's website for the exact dates and location of registration. Make sure to bring all important documents and some form of ID with you.

Once you have enrolled as a student at the University of Pannonia, you will need to find the immigration office. We recommend that students visit the immigration office with their student mentor. Students will receive more information about their enrolment procedure from the International Mobility Office.

Upon Arrival

The university organises welcoming events for its incoming international students. This is a great opportunity for meeting new people, learning more about your new “academic environment” and generally acclimatising yourself to your new surroundings. You may find it helpful to visit the respective international office’s website, where you can learn more about these events.

Study and Grading System: The academic year consists of two semesters, the autumn (September–January) and the spring (February-June) terms. Both semesters include a 14-week study period for lectures, seminars and practice and a 6-week examination period. Exams are concentrated in the 6-week exam period, but students have assignments and tests during the 14-week study period before their exams. At the beginning of each semester, students must register for their courses and at the end of the semester, they must register for their exams using the Neptun system. Training is based on a seminar/lab and lecture system. Seminars are generally smaller discussion groups with active student participation. In the seminar/lab, grades are usually awarded based on in-class performance, midterm and final papers, essays, oral presentations, or a combination of these requirements. Class attendance is mandatory for seminars. Lectures follow the typical model: a bigger audience, where students gain a broad and detailed knowledge of a particular discipline. As for lectures, class attendance is recommended. Students are awarded grades based on the results of oral and/or written examinations, which are conducted during the six-week examination period at the end of the academic term.

Neptun (Electronic Academic System): The NEPTUN Electronic Academic System is in use at all faculties of the university for educational administration. This electronic system is used to manage all administrative matters related to registration, courses, exams, tuition fees and stipends. All students, as well as administrators and lecturers throughout the University, use the Neptun system.

Credit System and the Methods of Assessment: The European Credit Transfer System (ECTS) was introduced into Hungarian legislation in September 2003. Students are assessed based on their learning and performance. The grading system is as follows: excellent (5), good (4), satisfactory (3), pass (2) and fail (1). A three-stage grading is also used in certain cases: excellent, satisfactory, and fail.

Support services, health and wellbeing: The process of settling in a new country entails a multitude of transitions, encompassing not only the social and cultural aspects of a foreign environment but also the more immediate changes in the surrounding environment, climate, diet and cultural norms. It is therefore essential that individuals take steps to ensure their well-being during this period of adjustment and that they have access to support networks to assist them in this endeavour.

Student Union: You will meet the Student Union at various events during your time at university, so it is helpful to know a little about the organisation. The Student Union is the official representative body of students at the University. Student affairs at the university level are the responsibility of the University Student Union (HÖK in Hungarian). They represent the interests of students in the highest decision-making bodies, administer university-level scholarships, dormitory places and organise major cultural and sporting events.

Student Mentoring System: We are proud to be able to offer our students the benefit of professional and personal development support from our mentors. Should you wish to do so, you can request a student mentor from the outset of your studies. This mentor will then be able to monitor your academic progress and individual development.

Besides personal consultations, we also provide guidance on a range of issues, including:

Settling into student life, includes advice on adapting to the academic and social environment of the university.

Recognising cultural changes, including advice on navigating cultural differences and adapting to the local culture.

Living in Veszprém, including advice on opening a bank account, navigating the local economy, and understanding the local housing market.

Health services, including advice on accessing healthcare and mental health services.

Academic issues, including advice on navigating the academic system and understanding the local academic culture.

Scientific Student Association

The Scientific Student Association has been active in Hungary for 70 years. Its essence is the cooperation and joint research activities of university lecturers/researchers and students, a special form of talent management in higher education.

Students can carry out independent research on a topic of their choice, with access to tools and instruments that go far beyond their academic studies. The teachers and researchers of the Faculty of Engineering will provide all the help they can to achieve their goals, whether it is by making use of the faculty's infrastructure, network of contacts, professional assistance or involvement in research groups. They can also take part in skills development courses, communication training and become part of an inspiring community.

Living in Veszprém

Students are drawn to Veszprém for a multitude of reasons. The city offers a relatively low cost of living, and it is perceived as a friendly, welcoming, and diverse student city.

If you have the opportunity, it might be a good idea to walk around campus after registration. This is a great way to get to know your new environment. It would be advisable to familiarise yourself with the student office, the international office, your department and the library, as these are likely to be the most important places for your time as an international student. It may be helpful to try to find them and memorise their location.

Veszprém has its public transport system which you can use if you buy a ticket or a pass. When starting your journey, you have to show your pass or punch your ticket. With a student card, you receive a discount on your monthly pass. Make sure you always have your student card with you while travelling to avoid getting fined.

Getting to other cities in Hungary

Getting to other cities in Hungary You can choose from the following travel options: railway, bus or car-sharing services. Students have a 50% concession for long-distance coach tickets and railway tickets, but again: always make sure to have a valid Hungarian student card.

Enjoying your student experience

Your top priority should be to make the most of your time at university, professionally and personally. It will be a pivotal chapter in your life, as your time as an international student may largely determine your future career path. The same goes for your young, open-minded and creative adult development.
Spending time in another country, studying in a language that is not necessarily your mother tongue and working with people from all over the world will greatly enrich your skills and intercultural competence. Both of these could prove crucial to your career.

Although you should indeed take the opportunity to travel and make lots of friends, achieving your academic goals should always be your priority.
